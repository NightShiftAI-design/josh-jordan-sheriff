Experience & Leadership

24 Years of Law Enforcement Service

Josh Jordan has served across corrections, patrol, school safety, supervision, investigations, and crisis response. He brings leadership experience and a proven commitment to professionalism and community trust.

Career Timeline

A career built from the ground up — with experience in both the Sheriff’s Office and local policing.

Early Service (Reserve)

While attending college, Josh volunteered as a Reserve Deputy with the Rhea County Sheriff’s Office and a Reserve Police Officer with the Spring City Police Department, gaining hands-on experience in patrol and jail operations.

Rhea County Sheriff’s Office

Began full-time law enforcement career in January 2004 as a Corrections Deputy, later promoted to Patrol Deputy, and became familiar with communities across Rhea County through road patrol and investigations.

Dayton Police Department

Joined Dayton PD in August 2006 and currently serves as a Patrol Sergeant, with responsibilities including supervision, officer training, and operational planning.

Leadership & Administration

Leadership is more than a title — it’s setting standards, training the next generation, and running operations effectively.

Patrol Sergeant

Supervises patrol operations, helps coordinate schedules, and supports departmental goals. Emphasizes respectful policing and balanced decision-making in the field.

Training & Mentorship

Trains new officers and shares both lessons learned and best practices so deputies can serve professionally, safely, and consistently.

Data & Compliance

Serves as Reporting Agency Coordinator for TBI crime data compliance and manages the department’s reporting database to support accuracy, transparency, and accountability.

Community Policing & School Safety

Josh’s experience includes dedicated time supporting schools and building trust through daily community interaction.

School Resource Experience

Served as a School Resource Officer at Bryan College, gaining valuable experience with campus and school safety, youth engagement, and prevention-focused policing.

Day-to-Day Patrol Work

Returned to road patrol because serving people directly is where his passion is — responding to calls, solving problems, and supporting victims in real time.

Balanced Enforcement

Believes in respect first, with the ability to be firm when the situation demands it — applying the right approach to each case.

Crisis Response & Mental Health

Crisis calls demand skill, empathy, and strong partnerships — especially when mental health is involved.

CIT Certified

Became a certified Crisis Intervention Team (CIT) officer in December 2023 and continues to strengthen crisis response skills.

Local Partnerships

Built working relationships with mental health providers including Rhea Mental Health and Volunteer Behavioral Health to improve outcomes and reduce harm.

De-escalation Focus

Training and experience emphasize modern de-escalation principles and safe, lawful resolution whenever possible.

Range of Investigations

Experience across the types of cases that impact families and neighborhoods — from everyday calls to major investigations.

Everyday Policing

Traffic enforcement, crashes (including fatal and hit-and-run investigations), thefts, burglaries, vandalism, and fraud/scams.

Serious & Violent Crime

Domestic violence, assaults, robbery investigations, rape investigations, homicide investigations, and crisis incidents.

Interagency Work

Joint investigations with agencies such as TBI and the U.S. Marshals, including cases involving exploitation and trafficking.

Training & Certifications (Highlights)

Josh has completed extensive training over his career, covering supervision, tactics, investigations, crisis response, and public safety.

Leadership & Supervision

Law enforcement management, supervision and leadership programs, policy development, and administration-focused training.

Investigations

Interviewing and interrogation techniques, intelligence gathering, internet crimes training, and specialized investigative coursework.

Tactics & Safety

Defensive tactics, patrol operations, DUI enforcement and SFST training, crisis response, and ongoing yearly weapons training.

Commitment to Professional Standards

Josh believes the Sheriff’s Office should continuously improve — through training, accountability, and leadership that sets high expectations. He’s committed to building an experienced command staff and a culture of professionalism that earns public trust.
