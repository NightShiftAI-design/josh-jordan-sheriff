Leadership Built From the Ground Up

Josh Jordan has served across nearly every part of day-to-day law enforcement and understands what it takes to run a complex public safety organization.

24 Years

Supervisor

CIT Certified

Career Timeline

Clear, straightforward progression — with service in both the Sheriff’s Office and Dayton PD.

College Years — Reserve Service

Volunteered as a Reserve Deputy with the Rhea County Sheriff’s Office and a Reserve Police Officer with the Spring City Police Department, gaining experience in road patrol and jail operations.

January 2004 — Rhea County Sheriff’s Office

Began full-time career as a Corrections Deputy, later promoted to Patrol Deputy. Served across multiple districts and became familiar with communities across the county.

August 2006 — Dayton Police Department

Joined Dayton PD and served in patrol roles before promoting into supervision. Also served as a School Resource Officer at Bryan College, bringing experience in campus safety and community engagement.

Today — Patrol Sergeant

Supervises patrol operations, trains new officers, supports scheduling and planning, and serves as the Reporting Agency Coordinator for TBI crime data compliance while managing the department’s report database.
