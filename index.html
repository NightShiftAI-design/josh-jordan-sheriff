About Josh Jordan

Josh Jordan is a lifelong resident of Rhea County and currently serves as a Patrol Sergeant with the Dayton Police Department. He’s running to bring experienced, professional leadership to the Rhea County Sheriff’s Office.

Local Roots

Lifelong Rhea County resident. Graduate of Rhea County High School (Class of 2000). Married and raising a family here at home.

Education

Associate degree in Business / General Management from Chattanooga State (graduated with honors), with coursework including public speaking, business law, accounting, finance, and statistics.

Public Service

Began serving early as a Reserve Officer and Reserve Deputy while in college, then built a full-time career in law enforcement serving Rhea County communities.

Experience & Leadership

Over 20 years in law enforcement with experience across patrol, corrections, supervision, investigations, training, and interagency coordination.

Career Path

Started full-time in 2004 as a Corrections Deputy with the Rhea County Sheriff’s Office, later served as Patrol Deputy, and since 2006 has served with the Dayton Police Department — now as a Patrol Sergeant.

Supervision

As a Patrol Sergeant: trains new officers, coordinates schedules, helps plan departmental goals, and emphasizes respectful policing with balanced decision-making.

Special Focus: Crisis Response

Certified Crisis Intervention Team (CIT) officer (December 2023) and works closely with local and regional mental health providers to improve outcomes in crisis situations.

Message on Leadership

Josh believes the Sheriff’s Office must be run with strong standards, clear accountability, and a culture of continuous improvement. That includes building a capable command team with operational experience across divisions (patrol, investigations, corrections, court security), so the office stays efficient, transparent, and responsive to the people of Rhea County.

Priorities

A safer Rhea County starts with professional leadership, community trust, and doing the work the right way — every day.

Public Safety & Response

Strengthen day-to-day patrol operations, improve response readiness, and keep focus on the crimes that most impact families and neighborhoods.

Accountability & Trust

Raise standards, enforce professionalism, and build stronger relationships with the public through transparency and respectful policing.

Training & Support

Invest in training, supervision, and leadership development to support deputies and ensure consistent, high-quality service countywide.

Crisis & Mental Health

Continue improving crisis response using CIT training and coordinated partnerships with mental health providers to reduce harm and improve outcomes.

Balanced, Fair Enforcement

Treat people with respect, use good judgment, and apply the right approach for each situation — firm when needed, fair always.

Strong Command Staff

Build an experienced leadership team with proven operational and administrative ability to manage a complex organization effectively.
