Core Priorities

These priorities reflect real-world experience in patrol, supervision, investigations, and crisis response — not theory or politics.

Professional Leadership

Lead from the front and set clear expectations across the Sheriff’s Office. Build a command structure with real operational experience to ensure every division functions effectively.

Accountability & Transparency

Maintain consistent standards, clear communication, and professional conduct. Accountability protects both the public and the deputies who serve them.

Strong Patrol Operations

Focus on the calls that most impact families and neighborhoods. Support deputies with training, supervision, and clear guidance in the field.

Balanced Enforcement

Treat people with respect until the situation demands otherwise. Apply sound judgment and lawful enforcement tailored to each circumstance.

Crisis & Mental Health Response

Expand CIT-informed response methods and strengthen partnerships with mental health providers to improve outcomes and reduce harm during crisis calls.

Continuous Improvement

A Sheriff’s Office that improves every day — through training, leadership development, and a culture that takes pride in doing the job correctly.

What This Means for Rhea County

Practical priorities that translate into safer communities and a more professional Sheriff’s Office.

Safer Communities

Deputies supported with training and supervision are better prepared to respond quickly, professionally, and safely.

Public Confidence

Clear standards and consistent leadership build trust between the Sheriff’s Office and the people it serves.

Stronger Organization

Effective leadership, accountability, and planning ensure the Sheriff’s Office operates as a cohesive team.

High Standards, Every Day

Josh Jordan does not believe in half measures. He believes strong leadership, accountability, and professionalism are essential to public safety and public trust.
